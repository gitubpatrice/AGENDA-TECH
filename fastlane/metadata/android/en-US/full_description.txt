Agenda Tech is a calendar for Android that keeps everything on your device. No cloud, no account, no ads, no trackers. It declares no internet permission at all, so it is technically unable to send anything anywhere — and you can check that yourself in Android's app settings.

It exists to let you leave Google Calendar without handing your appointments to anyone. A calendar says a lot about a person: their doctors, their lawyers, their job interviews, who they meet and when.

Features
========

• Four views — Month (swipe between months), Week, Day and Agenda list
• Events — all-day, description, per-event colour, postal address and GPS coordinates that open in your maps app (with no location permission)
• Recurring events (RFC 5545) — daily, weekly on chosen days, monthly, yearly; ending after N occurrences or on a date; edit or delete a single occurrence
• Reminders — exact alarms, re-armed after a reboot, free delay, your own sound, and a "snooze 10 min" button on the notification
• Encrypted backup — export your whole calendar to a password-protected .atbak file (Argon2-grade PBKDF2 at 600,000 iterations + AES-256-GCM). Keep it anywhere, cloud included: it stays unreadable without your password
• Search — by title, description, place, address or city; accents and case don't matter
• Import from your device calendar — copies what Google/Exchange already synced onto the phone, read-only, without any network
• .ics import / export (RFC 5545) through the system file picker
• Optional PIN and biometric lock
• Home-screen widgets, dark theme, French and English

Privacy
=======

• No INTERNET permission. Not "we promise not to" — the app cannot
• Encrypted at rest: SQLCipher (AES-256), master key sealed by the Android KeyStore
• Screenshots blocked (FLAG_SECURE), excluded from Android cloud backups
• Apache 2.0 — every promise on this page is checkable line by line

Limits, honestly
================

• Not a sync. Importing copies what is on the phone; an event created here does not travel back to Google. That is the price of zero network, and it is deliberate
• No CalDAV, no calendar sharing, no invitations — all of it needs a network
• The encrypted backup is the bridge between your devices, and it is a manual one
